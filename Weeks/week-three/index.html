From the SkillVet data, I created a program that parsed through study we found that most skills do not have any privacy policies, or they are broken. I cross-reference skill types to data types required by skills and then make a chart to what type of personal should be used for the honeytoken.  From that the most requested permissions were emails, device location, phone number, and zip code.

This was a confirmation of Jide S Edu SkillVet: Automated Traceability Analysis of Amazon Alexa Skills. The section covers this can be found in sections 3 to 3.3 of their study..

As a result of the study, we have narrowed the scope of skills we testing to United States, Germany, and France. We wanted to compare user data misusage between the United States privacy laws against skills that are required to follow General Data Protection Regulations (GDPR).
